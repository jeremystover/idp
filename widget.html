INDIVIDUAL DEVELOPMENT PLAN

Wait, what is this?

The ultimate step-by-step plan to help you navigate (and activate) your career growth at Gong and beyond.

Why should I use it?

Whether you’re 3, 15, or 30 years into your career– refining, refreshing, and acting on an individual development plan will help you optimize your career goals. This workbook will help you understand the skills, values, and career goals that will guide you through the next phases of your work. Hint: That goes way further than your next promotion.

How do I use it?

Just 5 steps. Complete them in order or skip as needed – it’s your plan. As you fill in each section, your completed plan will show up on the final page. You’ll receive next steps to turn the plan into action and are encouraged to refresh the plan every 6-12 months. We highly recommend sharing your final plan with your manager (if you’re comfortable).

I’d like to skip the exercises

Your plan, your choice! Feel free to skip around as needed.

IDENTIFY YOUR VALUES

Why this matters

our values are the guiding principles for your life. Even if you’ve never named them or said them out loud, they’re a part of who you are and how you make decisions. In fact, they’re tied to your levels of motivation and fulfillment. What really drives you? Once you can identify them, you can easily look for more opportunities, careers, and environments you’ll thrive in.

HIGHS AND LOWS

Life’s highs and lows have a sneaky way of signaling our values to us, which makes them a great values discovery tool. Here’s what we mean: If a career high was when you felt you were able to operate most independently, you might have a strong value of autonomy. On the other hand, perhaps a low was when your last company’s lack of efforts around diversity, equity, and inclusion left you feeling unsupported and unseen. This could suggest you have a core value of inclusion.

    CAREER VISION & GOALS

    Why this matters

    In this section, you’ll think big (way past your next promotion). While it’s very human to focus on promotion, compensation, and near term opportunities, our goal is to shift our mindset, and instead work holistically toward a values-centered future. This vision will be your north star, which is important to clarify even though it may grow and change throughout your career!

    DRAFT YOUR VISION

    Career visions are: The big, pivotal moments in your career that you’re reaching for. Think of them as the answer to the question “Where are you going in your career?” Your vision should be aspirational and serve as a compass to guide you on your career journey, supporting you in filtering difficult life choices. For this exercise, we recommend your vision be no less than 7-10 years in the future - further out is ok, too! We encourage you to include elements from the rest of your life in this vision, as they are all intertwined (family, living location, hobbies, etc.)!

    Career visions are not: Short-term goals, like the next promotion. Visions also don’t get caught up in the details, so try to stop yourself from asking “how will I get there?” at this stage (though it’s tempting!)

    RUN CAREER EXPERIMENTS

    Why this matters

    It’s time to beta-test your life – start with creating a small career experiment for each of your goals to safely test our new skills on your path to your career vision. Remember: It’s okay to sometimes fail when trying something new.

    DESIGN YOUR EXPERIMENTS

    For each of your goals, design 1-3 experiments. This is an opportunity for you to reach for your goals through small, controlled steps, ensuring they are aligned with your larger vision and values. Once you design an experiment, define success and prioritize using the framework below

    CRITERIA FOR YOUR EXPERIMENTS

    • They should be connected to your vision and goals
    • Start small! Keep it low-cost in terms of time, energy, money.
    • Make it a SMART goal: is it specific, measurable, attainable, realistic and time-bound?

    EVALUATE YOUR EXPERIMENTS

    Before you run them, utilize the 3 Cs:

    • Confidence: How confident do you feel that you will actually run the experiment?
    • Coherence: Does it make sense? Is it consistent with your Vision, goals, values, etc.? Is it aligned with the resources you have? Consider bringing in a trusted colleague, mentor, or manager to help you gauge this.
    • Constraints: What do you need to overcome to make this happen? I.e. my bandwidth, my own inner critic, gaps in my network

    After you run them, ask yourself:

    • What did you learn from the experiment?
    • Is there an opportunity for you to expand on it, or refine it?
    • Would it be more worthwhile to put your energy into something else?
